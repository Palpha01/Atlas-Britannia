General information

Scotland is a country in the north of Great Britain, forming part of the United Kingdom. It is known for its diverse landscapes, including mountains, forests, beaches, rivers, and lochs (lakes).

The country has a rich history and cultural heritage, with significant contributions to literature, music, and sports. The capital city is Edinburgh, while Glasgow is the largest city.

Scotland has a population of about 5.4 million people, with most living in the southern part of the country, particularly in the Central Belt, which includes cities like Edinburgh, Glasgow, and Dundee.

Demographics

Scotland had a population of 5,463,300 in 2019, and by 2022, it was estimated to be around 5,439,842.

Around 70% of the country's population (3.5 million) live in the Central Belt, a region stretching between Edinburgh and Glasgow.

The largest ethnic group is "White: Scottish," comprising 83.9% of the population in 2011.

Eatery

Haggis is the national Scottish dish that consists of sheep's heart, liver, and lungs minced with suet, onions, oatmeal, and seasonings, boiled in the stomach of the animal. It is served with neeps (mashed turnip) and tatties (mashed potato) and is often accompanied by a whisky.
Irn-Bru is a Scottish carbonated soft drink often described as "Scotland's other national drink" after Scotch whisky. Introduced in 1901, it is produced by A.G. Barr, a company based in Glasgow, and is manufactured in Westfield, Cumbernauld, North Lanarkshire.

Traditions

Bagpipes are a woodwind instrument that uses enclosed reeds fed from a constant reservoir of air in the form of a bag. Bagpipes are known for their continuous sound, as they are open-ended and do not allow the player to stop the pipe from sounding.
Hogmanay, also known as New Year's Eve, is a significant celebration in Scotland and parts of northern England. It is marked by various traditions and festivities that last from December 31 to January 1, and sometimes even into January 2. One of the notable traditions is the singing of "Auld Lang Syne" at midnight, which originated during Hogmanay and has since become a worldwide custom

Landmarks

Edinburgh Castle is part of the Old and New Towns of Edinburgh World Heritage Site and is home to the Honours of Scotland, the oldest Crown jewels in Britain. The castle, in the care of Historic Environment Scotland, is Scotland's most visited paid tourist attraction, with over 2.2 million visitors in 2019 and over 70 percent of leisure visitors to Edinburgh visiting the castle.
Loch Ness is a large freshwater lake located near Inverness in the Scottish Highlands. Loch Ness is renowned for its mysterious waters and is the site of numerous sightings of a creature known as the Loch Ness Monster, or Nessie. The legend of the monster dates back to the 6th century, with the first written account appearing in a biography of Saint Columba.

Fun Fact: Invention of tikka masala

The world-famous dish chicken tikka masala is said to have been first created in Glasgow by a Pakistani chef named Ali Ahmed Aslam.

According to a popular theory, Aslam added tomato soup and spices to a mix of yogurt and cream to make the curry after a customer complained that his food was too dry.
