General information

Northern Ireland is one of the four countries of the United Kingdom, located in the northeast of the island of Ireland.

It was created as a separate legal entity on 3 May 1921 under the Government of Ireland Act 1920, comprising six of the nine counties of Ulster: Antrim, Armagh, Down, Fermanagh, Londonderry, and Tyrone. The capital city of Northern Ireland is Belfast, which is also its largest city and an important port.

Northern Ireland is known for its natural beauty, including the Giant's Causeway, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and Lough Neagh, the largest freshwater lake in the British Isles. Northern Ireland has a population of around 1.9 million people, making up about 3% of the UK's population and 27% of the population on the island of Ireland.

Demographics

Northern Ireland had a population of 1,903,175 as of the 2021 census. The population density is 133 people per square kilometre.

The majority of the population identifies as White, comprising over 97%. Approximately 95.37% of the population speaks English as their first language, while 0.32% speak Irish as their main home language.

Eatery

Guinness is a stout that originated in the brewery of Arthur Guinness at St. James's Gate, Dublin, Ireland, in the 18th century. It is now owned by the British-based multinational alcoholic beverage maker Diageo. Guinness is known for its innovative advertising and has been a significant part of Irish culture and heritage. The company has a long history of philanthropy and has supported its employees and communities for over 200 years.
The Ulster Fry is a hearty breakfast dish originating from Northern Ireland. It typically includes fried bacon, sausages, black pudding, mushrooms, tomato, and eggs served with fried soda bread and potato farls. Variations of the Ulster Fry can be found throughout the day in Northern Ireland, not just at breakfast time. It is a substantial meal that traditionally provided enough calories for a morning's labor.

Traditions

Republic of Ireland's national St. Patrick's Day is still celebrated in Ulster, a region in the United Kingdom that includes Northern Ireland and parts of the Republic of Ireland. The Ulster American Folk Park in Omagh, Northern Ireland, hosts traditional St. Patrick's Day celebrations, including live music, Irish dance performances, and craft demonstrations. The event usually takes place on March 16th and 17th, but due to the current date being May 31, 2025, these celebrations have already occurred.
Ulster Rugby is one of the four professional provincial rugby union teams from the island of Ireland. They compete in the Irish regional pool of the United Rugby Championship and in the European Rugby Champions Cup, each of which they have won once. Ulster were the first Irish team and the first team outside England and France to win the European Cup in 1999.

Landmarks

The Giant's Causeway is a natural wonder located in County Antrim, Northern Ireland, and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is known for its unique formation of approximately 40,000 hexagonal basalt columns that jut out of the cliff faces, resembling stepping stones leading into the sea.
The Dark Hedges, located on Bregagh Road between Armoy and Stranocum in County Antrim, Northern Ireland, is an avenue of beech trees that form an atmospheric tunnel, making it one of the most photographed natural phenomena in Northern Ireland.
